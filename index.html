WHAT IS ASD?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a developmental disorder that affects social communication, behavior, and interaction. It is referred to as a "spectrum" because it manifests in different ways and varies in severity. Key characteristics include:

Social Communication Challenges
Difficulty understanding social cues, making eye contact and engaging in conversations.

Repetitive Behaviours
Movements like “Hand Flapping”, Routines or Intense Interests in Topics.

Sensory Sensitivities
Heightened or Decreased Sensitivity to Sensory Input.

Difficulty with Transitions
Changes in routine or environment can be challenging, causing anxiety or frustration.

HOW CAN I HELP?

When interacting with children with autism, patience, consistency, and empathy are key. Here are some effective methods for engaging with them:

Structured Environment
Create a Predictable Environment with Routines and Visual Schedules.

Clear Communication
Use simple language and provide visual aids to convey instructions and expectations.

Social Skills Training
Teaches children how to initiate, maintain and end conversations as well as understanding social cues and emotions.

Positive Reinforcement
Encourage desirable behaviours, praise and small incentives can be used to reinforce efforts and progress.
